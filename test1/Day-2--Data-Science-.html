All these plots can tell us visually if the data is normally distributed. Another method is to test with Shapiro Wilk’s method

shapiro.test(iris$Sepal.Length)
## 
##  Shapiro-Wilk normality test
## 
## data:  iris$Sepal.Length
## W = 0.97609, p-value = 0.01018

Hypothesis: The data is normal. If P-value < 0.05 then we will reject our hypothesis. From the output, the p-value > 0.05 implying that the distribution of the data are not significantly different from normal distribution. In other words, we can say that our data is normal.
